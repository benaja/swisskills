Part four - Design skills

Total points: 67

General information

In part four you will prove your design skills. Therefore you will create a design for a mobile application. Your working space is the folder work/part_four/.

You can only change or create new files under work/part_four/. All other files will be reverted for the review process.

Task 1 - Mobile Calendar View

Create a mobile design for a calendar day view using your favorite tool (e.g. Adobe Photoshop). The design has to meet professional visual expectations.

  • Screen dimensions

    You have to design for the Samsung Galaxy S5 screen only:

    • 1920x1080px
    • Portrait format only!

    Use the Photoshop template file (template.psd) provided.

  • Color scheme

    For your design you have to choose four to six colors. Define your color scheme and place the colors used in the following color scheme file:

    color-scheme.html

    You have to modify the CSS in the color-scheme.html file according to the comments.

The following data has to be displayed:

  • General information:

    • Day number: 23
    • Day name: Wednesday
    • Month and year: September 2015

  • Action buttons: Create the following icon buttons:

    • A button to create a new calendar entry
    • A button to show the whole month
    • Buttons to change day (back and forth)

    You are expected to design necessary icons by yourself and are not allowed to use existing icons (e.g. fontawesome).

  • Calendar entries: 3 to 5 calendar entries have to be visible within the phones viewport (without scrolling). Each calendar entry has to contain a title, a time, a location and the attendees pictures. You can choose the items from the following list:

    Title Time Location Attendees
    Video Conference 8:15 AM Zurich Julia, Pascal, Dana, Jasmin
    Daily Standup 10:30 AM Zurich Manuel, Julian
    Mark's Birthday Meal 11:30 AM Lilys Restaurant Mark, Michael, Laura, Noora, Hans, +5
    Gala Night 4:00 PM Olten Emma, Kristiana
    Gala Apero 8:00 PM Olten Emma, Kristiana
    Train Home 11:00 PM Olten Julia

    Use pictures for attendees not their names. You can choose avatars from these pictures: faces1, faces2, faces3

    Create and use icons for the time and location.

The customer asked to get the RAW design file and also a PNG version which he can print and show his stakeholders.

  • Expected result

    The design files (RAW and PNG), placed under work/part_four/design/

Marking scheme

Objective 29

  • 2 The color scheme is defined in color-scheme.html (1) and 4-6 colors are chosen (1).
  • 2 The design files (RAW and PNG) are placed under /work/part_four/design/.
  • 3 The Photoshop template is used.
  • 3 All general information is visible: Day number (1), day name (1), month (0.5) and year (0.5).
  • 8 A button to create (1), to view month (1) and to go back and forth (2) are displayed. Icons are designed for all buttons (1 for each icon).
  • 3 3-5 calendar entries are visible within the phones viewport (without scrolling). Minus one point for each less or more displayed.
  • 6 Each calendar entry contains a title (1), time (1), location (1) and the attendees pictures (3).
  • 2 An icon is used for the time (1) and for the location (1).
  • -6 No existing icons are copied (e.g. fontawesome), otherwise -6 points.

Subjective 38

  • 6 Impression: Colors used and theme are appropriate for the target audience.
  • 6 Composition: Design elements fit together and are relevant.
  • 6 Creativity: Design is creative (2), suprising (2) and innovative (2).
  • 6 Graphic work: Pictures are prepared neatly and integrated professionally.
  • 5 Usability: The design is user friendly, efficient and engaging (2). It is clear on which page I am (1). All interaction elements can be easily identified and their purpose is obvious (2).
  • 9 Readability: The text is well readable: contrast (1), font-size (1) and line-height (1). Inner and outer distances to their adjacent elements shows at what element the text belongs (3). Text is readable by color-blind readers (e.g. distinction between red and green) (3).
